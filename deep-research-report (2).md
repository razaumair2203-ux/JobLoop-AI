## How the score should be generated

Do **not** generate one opaque score and call it done. The strongest design is a **score stack** with three separate outputs: **Fit Score**, **Confidence Score**, and **CV Quality Score**, plus one optional combined **Application Readiness Score**. That structure matches both the research direction and market behavior: ConFit shows how to do scalable resumeÔÇôjob matching with dense representations; more recent work describes dual-encoder retrieval followed by richer interaction models such as cross-encoders and knowledge-graph-informed reranking; and products like ţłÇurlţłéTealţłéturn4search0ţłü, ţłÇurlţłéReziţłéturn4search2ţłü, and ţłÇurlţłéResume Wordedţłéturn5search1ţłü already expose resume-analysis or scoring surfaces that users understand. ţłÇciteţłéturn7view6ţłéturn18view1ţłéturn19view1ţłéturn4search4ţłéturn7view11ţłéturn7view14ţłü

A strong production default is this:

**Application Readiness Score** = `Gate ├Ś (0.65 ├Ś Fit Score + 0.20 ├Ś Confidence Score + 0.15 ├Ś CV Quality Score)`

The `Gate` is critical. If a role has an explicit must-haveÔÇöwork authorization, license, location constraint, language requirement, clearance, mandatory degree, required years of experience, or hard technical prerequisiteÔÇöand that requirement is clearly missing, the final score should be capped or marked ÔÇťnot currently eligible.ÔÇŁ If the requirement is unresolved rather than clearly missing, cap the score less aggressively and trigger a clarification question instead of silently failing the candidate. This is an inference from the literature and current products: scalable matching works best when coarse filtering, semantic similarity, and explainable reranking are separated rather than collapsed into one hidden number. ţłÇciteţłéturn7view6ţłéturn18view1ţłéturn19view1ţłéturn19view2ţłü

Inside the **Fit Score**, I would start with this weighting scheme and then let the AutoResearch loop tune it:

- **Requirement and skill coverage**: 35%
- **Responsibility overlap**: 20%
- **Experience depth and recency**: 15%
- **Title and seniority alignment**: 10%
- **Quantified achievements and outcomes**: 10%
- **Logistics fit** such as location, work mode, compensation band, or authorization: 10%

The matching mechanics should also be two-stage. First, parse the JD into a structured requirement graph with required/preferred labels and importance weights. Then compute a fast first-pass score using normalized exact matches plus semantic retrieval over the candidate cloud. After that, apply a slower reranker on the top results to capture finer interactions between resume evidence and job context. ConFit supports the fast dense-retrieval side, while newer work explicitly describes the common dual-encoder-plus-cross-encoder pattern and argues for explainable, factor-wise reranking. ţłÇciteţłéturn7view6ţłéturn18view1ţłéturn19view1ţłéturn19view2ţłü

The **Confidence Score** should be driven by **provenance**, not by how polished the text sounds. Every fact in the candidate cloud should carry source pointers, timestamps, agreement/disagreement across uploaded CVs, and a status such as *user-asserted*, *inferred*, *verified by document*, or *confirmed by user after conflict*. A fact that appears consistently across multiple CVs and is backed by a proof document should score materially higher than a fact that appears once in a single uploaded resume. Contradictions should reduce confidence and trigger a targeted question rather than being averaged away. This is one of the most important differentiators you can build, because recent extraction work emphasizes field-level comparison and evaluation, not just free-form text generation. ţłÇciteţłéturn18view2ţłéturn22view0ţłéturn22view1ţłü

The **CV Quality Score** should stay narrow and honest. It should reflect parseability, section completeness, quantified outcomes, specificity of bullets, and redundancy; it should **not** dominate actual job fit. Current products like ţłÇurlţłéReziţłéturn4search2ţłü, ţłÇurlţłéTealţłéturn4search0ţłü, and ţłÇurlţłéResume Wordedţłéturn5search1ţłü all signal that users value resume grading, ATS-readiness, and job-description analysis. Your improvement is to make it explicit that ÔÇťgood document qualityÔÇŁ is separate from ÔÇťstrong role fitÔÇŁ and separate again from ÔÇťhigh evidence confidence.ÔÇŁ ţłÇciteţłéturn4search4ţłéturn7view11ţłéturn7view14ţłü

## Product workflow from multi-CV intake to job-specific CV

Your ingestion pipeline should create a **career evidence graph**, not just extract text from a single document. That is especially important because recent resume-extraction research emphasizes that resumes are highly heterogeneous in layout and that robust extraction often needs hybrid PDF metadata plus OCR plus layout-aware regeneration before structured extraction. Commercial parsing vendors make the same point in different language: parse the original file whenever possible and return normalized structured fields for downstream logic. ţłÇciteţłéturn18view2ţłéturn6search5ţłéturn17view2ţłéturn7view15ţłü

Once one or more CVs are parsed, normalize them into canonical entities such as jobs, employers, skills, education records, certifications, projects, achievements, languages, locations, links, and timeline intervals. Then compare them field by field. If two CVs disagree on a date range, title, degree, or certification, do not choose randomly. Create a conflict object with severity, possible impact on the score, and suggested evidence needed to resolve it. That turns ÔÇťmessy user historyÔÇŁ into ÔÇťstructured uncertainty,ÔÇŁ which is a much more valuable product asset.

The clarification engine should ask **fewer, higher-value questions**. AnthropicÔÇÖs MCP elicitation flow is a useful model here because it supports structured mid-task dialogs with requested schemas instead of vague back-and-forth text. In your app, every question should be targeted at reducing uncertainty that materially changes fit, confidence, or eligibility. Asking ÔÇťWhich employment end date is correct?ÔÇŁ is useful if it changes years-of-experience or recency. Asking ÔÇťWould you like a nicer summary sentence?ÔÇŁ is much less useful during profile formation. ţłÇciteţłéturn22view0ţłéturn22view1ţłü

When a JD is posted, parse it into a requirement graph, score current evidence against that graph, and then ask **gap-closing enrichment questions**. Questions should be of the form: ÔÇťI found evidence for X but not for Y; have you actually done Y, and if yes, can you provide the project, proof, and outcome?ÔÇŁ That is a better experience than immediate rewriting because it first improves the underlying candidate cloud. Only after that should the system generate a targeted CV. The rule should be simple: **verified or user-confirmed facts can go straight into the CV; inferred but unverified facts should appear as suggestions, not automatic claims**. ţłÇciteţłéturn18view1ţłéturn19view1ţłéturn19view2ţłü

If you want this to become a platform that other businesses can plug into, the cleanest API surface is five services: **ingest**, **profile/evidence**, **job parse**, **match score**, and **CV render**. The most valuable endpoint is not ÔÇťwrite me a resume.ÔÇŁ It is ÔÇťreturn the candidate cloud, provenance, unresolved conflicts, missing evidence, and the explanation-backed score object.ÔÇŁ That makes your system useful to career apps, staffing firms, coaching platforms, job boards, and internal talent tools.

## Evaluation, privacy, and fairness guardrails

The moment your API is used downstream by employers, staffing firms, or screening systems, this becomes not just a product problem but a governance problem. The EEOC states that AI and other automated technologies are used in recruiting, screening, and hiring and warns that seemingly neutral practices can still create unlawful disparate impact; NISTÔÇÖs bias guidance explicitly argues for a socio-technical approach to identifying and managing bias. ţłÇciteţłéturn7view9ţłéturn7view8ţłü

That means some rules should be non-negotiable. Protected attributes should never be direct inputs to the fit score. Every score should return its factor breakdown and supporting evidence. You should preserve user override and correction paths. You should keep a blind-review mode for business customers. You should version prompts, schemas, and models. You should run subgroup audits on score distributions and explanation quality. And you should expose deletion/export controls because retention and reuse of candidate evidence is itself a trust issue, especially when stored files persist until explicitly deleted. ţłÇciteţłéturn7view8ţłéturn7view9ţłéturn22view3ţłéturn12view1ţłü

The real moat, then, is not prettier wording or faster one-shot tailoring. It is a **calibrated evidence graph**, an **explainable fit score**, and a **Karpathy-style optimization loop** that keeps improving extraction, questioning, and rendering against a hard evaluation suite.
